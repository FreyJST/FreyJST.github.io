Analogue creature in a digital world

I created this site in 2016, around the same time I came to understand the implications of the Cambridge Analytica scandal. I was horrified that the websites that had helped me develop social skills and cultivate a social life offline had been harvesting data about me like the robots in the fucking Matrix. This site was the beginning of me re-discovering my love for the internet, social media (in my view the whole internet is media that is social because that is the fucking point), and technology. I had always been interested in hand coded HTML sites made in Notepad and the like, but it wasn't until I discovered Neocities that I found a place to actually play with them, as well as meet others who were interested in them as well.

I have a bad habit of changing my site name and fucking up everyone's links whenever I have another aesthetic identity crisis. The last two years or so I haven't had as much motivation or energy to keep overhauling my website on a spur of the moment, so it's given me time to actually think about what I want it to look like, and what I want it to do. I like so many different things it's hard to get them to work together sometimes.

Formally known as Neon Garden, Psyche64 (or some combination of the two), flowerfuck, and Pink Sushi. Est 2017.
